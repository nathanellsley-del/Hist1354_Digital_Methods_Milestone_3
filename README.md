The second hurdle involved properly displaying the data. This was my first time using loops with matplotlib.pyplot to generate a scatterplot that incorporated multiple variables; I needed to account for the state, median personal income, and percentage of veterans with higher education. While I eventually got the code to run, the visualization itself was not clear or effective. To improve this, I asked generative AI about alternative tools and they recommended plotly.express, which allowed me to create a more interactive graph that better communicated the relationship between variables. 

Based on my preliminary analysis, the data shows a positive correlation between education and income among Vietnam veterans because as the percentage of veterans in a state with a bachelor’s degree or higher increases, the median personal income in that state also tends to rise. This relationship aligns with broader economic patterns observed in the United States, particularly during the late 20th century, when higher education was strongly associated with access to higher-paying careers. Although this is only a preliminary analysis, the trend suggests that Vietnam veterans followed similar socioeconomic patterns as the general population.
